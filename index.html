About Me and My Journey:

Currently, I am pursuing my master's degree in Computer Science at Northeastern University in San Jose, California, which I expect to complete in Dec 2024.

Before joining Northeastern University, I worked as a data engineer at Beijing Institute of Technology New Energy Information Technology Co., Ltd. During my tenure, I developed a data pipeline using Pandas, NumPy, and SQL to handle missing and abnormal values of battery data and trained models using LSTM, CNN, Linear Regression, and Prophet Framework to predict the battery life of vehicles. I also analyzed the retire time of batteries in vehicles from different fields, such as the production year, sales place, and use purpose of the vehicles. In addition, I have experience as a software development engineer at Sky blue Technology Co., Ltd, where I created the SMS verification code feature, implemented an encrypted data function, realized third-party platform payments, improved user response speed, and designed and implemented a data analysis feature that counts users’ click number, collections, inventory, and sales of goods.

I have also worked on various projects, including a student registration system that enables students and professors to search and manage their courses online using Django, AJAX, and MySQL. I also developed the Lyrics Translate player, which is an Android application that helps users find the lyrics of songs by Spotify's API interface using Android Studio and Firebase.

Apart from my professional experience, I have been awarded scholarships five times for my outstanding academic performance.Currently, I am serving as an instructional assistant at Northeastern University, collaborating with professors in completing teaching tasks in Data Visualization and Mixed Reality course while serving a global student population of 30+ global students across four classes. I have also served as a teaching assistant, providing academic support to students by answering questions and grading assignments for the Objected Oriented Design course.

Finally, I am the inventor of a pending patent titled "Method for Predicting Power Battery Retirement Based on Capacity Decay" and I am passionate about using my skills and experience to help organizations achieve their goals.

My Value: I am always curious about technology, never hesitate to ask, willing to learn, and take feedbacks seriously
